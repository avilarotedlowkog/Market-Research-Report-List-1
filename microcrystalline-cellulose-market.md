<p><p>The microcrystalline cellulose market is divided into two main types: wood-based and non-wood-based. Wood-based microcrystalline cellulose is derived from pulp and is commonly sourced from softwood or hardwood trees, offering high purity and desirable functional properties. In contrast, non-wood-based microcrystalline cellulose is obtained from agricultural byproducts like cotton, corn, or rice. This alternative source provides a sustainable option and often caters to niche applications. Each type serves various industries, including pharmaceuticals, food, and cosmetics, based on specific performance requirements.</p></p>
