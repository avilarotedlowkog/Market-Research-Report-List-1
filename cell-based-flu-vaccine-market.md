<p><p>The cell-based flu vaccine market consists primarily of two types: trivalent and quadrivalent influenza vaccines. Trivalent vaccines target three virus strains, typically covering two A strains and one B strain, offering broad protection. In contrast, quadrivalent vaccines expand this coverage by including an additional B strain, thus enhancing effectiveness against circulating viruses. Both types leverage cell culture technology for faster production processes compared to traditional egg-based methods, allowing for more accurate strain matching and improved vaccine availability during flu seasons.</p></p>
